Ultimate Cross Rulebook

Winning the Game

There are two ways to win in Ultimate Cross:

  • Reduce your opponent’s Ruler’s DEF to 0.
  • Force your opponent to draw from an empty deck.

Card Anatomy

Every card in Ultimate Cross has these core elements:

  1. Name — the card’s title.
  2. Type — the card’s classification (Ruler, Creature, Equipment, Hazard, etc.).
  3. Cost — how much Aura is required to play the card.
  4. Effect — what the card does when played or when activated.
  5. Flavor Text — extra story or theme text with no gameplay effect.
  6. Artist Credit — who created the card art.
  7. Rarity — how rare the card is in the set.
  8. Card Number — the card’s place within the set.

Rulers, Creatures, and Equipments also show ATK and DEF.

Field and Zone Layout

The game board is divided into these main zones:

  • Field — cards currently in play. You may have up to 7 Creatures and up to 5 Equipments/Hazards on your field.
  • Deck — your draw pile.
  • Graveyard — for used, discarded, or destroyed cards.
  • Erase — for erased cards. These cards never return to the game.

Game Setup

  1. Place your Ruler into your Ruler Zone.
  2. Shuffle your deck and draw the top 6 cards.
  3. Randomly determine who goes first.
  4. First player draws 1 card on their first turn and starts with 2 Aura. Second player draws no cards on their first turn and starts with 3 Aura.

Aura

Aura is the main resource of the game. It is used to play cards and activate effects.

  • At the start of each turn, increase your Max Aura by 1.
  • Then reset your current Aura to your Max Aura.
  • Each player can have a maximum of 10 Aura.
  • When you play a card, you pay its play cost in Aura.
  • Unused Aura carries over to your opponent’s turn.

Turn Structure

Each turn is divided into phases:

  1. Draw Phase
    • Draw 1 card.
    • Reset your Aura to Max Aura.
    • Gain 1 additional Aura.
  2. Main Phase
    • Play cards from your hand.
    • Activate ACT effects.
    • Declare attacks.
  3. End Phase
    • Remove any effects that expire at end of turn.
    • Remove Immobile 1 from your cards.
    • End your turn and pass priority to your opponent.

Playing Cards

  1. Choose a card from your hand.
  2. Pay Aura equal to its play cost.
  3. Place the card onto the field face-up. If it is a Hazard, place it face-down instead.

When a Creature enters the field, it gains Immobile 1, which prevents it from attacking on that same turn.

Card Resolution

  1. The turn player plays a card.
  2. The opposing player may activate and resolve any response effects available at that time.
  3. The turn player resolves any effects that trigger when a card is played.

If any cards or effects leave the field before the resolution completes, the affected effect or attack does not resolve.

Effect Types

  • AUTO — triggers automatically when its condition is met.
  • ACT — can be used only during the Main Phase. Declare the effect to use it. Limited to once per turn per card name.
  • CONT — continuous. It remains active while the card remains on the field.

Activating Effects

  1. Turn player activates an effect.
  2. Opposing player may activate a response effect if available.
  3. Turn player resolves the activated effect.

If cards or effects leave the field before resolution, the triggering effect does not resolve.

Attacking

  1. Declare an attacker and its target.
  2. If the opponent has no Creatures, you may attack their Ruler directly.
  3. The opposing player may activate response effects.
  4. The attacking player may activate effects that respond to attacking.
  5. Resolve damage.

Damage Resolution

  1. Both targets lose DEF equal to the opponent’s ATK.
  2. If a card’s DEF reaches 0 or less, it is destroyed and sent to the Graveyard.
  3. If a card attacks a Ruler, the Ruler takes damage equal to the attacking card’s ATK, and the attacking card loses DEF equal to the Ruler’s ATK.
  4. If a Ruler’s DEF reaches 0 or less, that player loses the game.

Deck Construction

  • Your deck must contain 1 Ruler and 40–50 cards.
  • You may include up to 4 copies of the same card name.
